Seating

The 2021 Dodge Challenger has a nice-looking interior, but it isn't as upscale as those of some rivals. Trunk space and rear-seat room are excellent, and the infotainment system is intuitive.

The Uconnect infotainment system is easy to use and straightforward, with a simply menu structure. Most graphics look good, but the display feels a little outdated compared to more modern systems.Optional features include an 8.4-inch touch screen, navigation, satellite radio, a six- or nine-speaker Alpine premium stereo, an 18-speaker Harman Kardon surround-sound stereo, a sunroof, remote start, and a universal garage door opener.

Interior Features

The five-seat Dodge Challenger has one of the roomiest cabins in the class, with comfortable front seats and impressive rear legroom. Cloth upholstery, a six-way power-adjustable driver's seat, and a leather-wrapped steering wheel and shift knob are standard.

Options include heated and ventilated front seats, driver's seat and steering wheel memory settings, upgraded cloth upholstery, Nappa leather upholstery, Nappa leather and Alcantara suede upholstery, and Laguna leather upholstery. You can also opt for a power tilt-and-telescoping heated steering wheel with a flat-bottom design and suede-wrapping.

"Contemporary yet with retro touches, the driver-oriented layout can house an 8.4-inch infotainment touchscreen as well as a sophisticated information center between the main gauges."

Kelley Blue Book
